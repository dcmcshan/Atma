How It Works

Distributed audio architecture for immersive experiences

1

Audio Input

Audio streams from Bluetooth A2DP or Wi-Fi to a master node. The master decodes and prepares the audio stream for distribution.

2

Spatial Encoding

The master node encodes audio into First-Order Ambisonics (FOA) format with W, X, Y, Z channels, representing the full 3D soundfield.

3

Network Distribution

Audio packets are transmitted over Wi-Fi UDP multicast to all nodes. Control signals manage synchronization and spatial positioning.

4

Local Processing

Each ESP32 node receives the FOA stream, performs local spatial decoding, applies EQ and filtering, then outputs to its speakers.

5

Synchronized Playback

All nodes maintain synchronized sample clocks using timestamps, ensuring phase-coherent playback across the entire network.

6

Immersive Experience

The result is a distributed sound field where ambient textures, voices, and sounds move fluidly from jar to jar, creating a magical audio ecosystem.
