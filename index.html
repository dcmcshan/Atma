What is Atma?

A spatial audio field kit disguised as a lantern.

Atma is a networked ambisonic speaker that turns mason jars into programmable sound lanterns. Each vessel hides a dual-driver isobaric stack, ESP32 brain, and weather-ready enclosure. Multiple jars synchronize wirelessly to paint a living sound field indoors or outdoors.

Dual-driver core

Opposed 2" drivers deliver surprising low-end inside a jar footprint.

Isomorphic scenes

Author once and deploy to trails, domes, or rituals with location-aware fades.

Open toolkit

Wi-Fi/UDP sync, sensor hooks, and scene templates in a single pelican case.

Why Ambisonics?

FOA lets sound travel like weather.

First-Order Ambisonics (FOA) encodes the entire 3D soundfield into WXYZ channels. Atma decodes locally, so a single stream can power dozens of jars without a central array or cabled infrastructure.

  • Phase-coherent drift as textures move jar to jar.
  • Swap scenes without rewiring the physical layout.
  • Future-proof for higher-order expansion and AR overlays.

Why a mason jar?

The vessel is part lantern, part resonant chamber.

The wide-mouth mason jar is iconic, airtight, and acoustically predictable. It protects electronics, diffuses LEDs, and feels at home in forests or sanctuaries. Stainless rods double as structure and wiring, while the lid becomes a mesh diffuser for touch, light, or weather data.

Lantern lineage

Reference tea lanterns, fireflies, and ritual vessels. Each jar glows with the sound it emits.

Durable shell

Gasketed lid, stainless hardware, and internal baffles keep water, soil, and curious hands out.

Acoustic benefit

Cylindrical enclosure supports opposing drivers for noise cancellation and deep bass.
